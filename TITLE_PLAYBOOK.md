# BEG SEO Title Playbook

Standing rule for every `<title>` tag on beghr.com. Applies to metadata.title, openGraph.title, and twitter.title (keep all three identical per page).

## The formula
`Primary keyword first  +  ONE hook matched to intent  +  | BEG`
- Under ~60 characters (so Google does not truncate).
- Keyword in the first ~50 chars. The hook never crowds out the keyword.
- One hook per title, never two.
- No em-dashes or en-dashes. "isolved" always lowercase.

## Two intents, two hooks (this is the whole game)

### 1. Commercial pages -> OFFER hook
Pages: service hubs, pricing, industry service pages, vs-competitor / alternative / switch pages.
The searcher wants to buy or compare, so the offer matches intent and jumps out.
- Managed payroll -> "Instant Quote" or "Instant Payroll Quote"
- Job placement / legal -> "Instant Fee Quote"
- HCM software -> "Instant Price Quote"

Status: DONE. 3 hubs, pricing, 6 payroll competitor pages, 182 sub-pages.

### 2. Informational pages -> CURIOSITY / SPECIFICITY hook (NOT a CTA)
Pages: blog posts, guides, how-tos, cost explainers, "what is" and "vs" explainers.
The searcher wants an answer. A sales CTA here reads as an ad and LOWERS clicks. Grab attention with specificity instead.

Hook toolkit (rotate across pages, do not repeat one phrase site-wide):
- Real number / range / year: "The Real Cost", "in 2026", a specific dollar figure
- Bracketed value-add: "(Full Breakdown)", "[+ Free Calculator]", "[2026 Data]"
- Contrarian / insider: "What Nobody Tells You", "The Truth About", "...Actually..."
- Outcome / stakes: "...Without Overpaying", "...Before You Sign", "...That Actually Fills"
- Named segment: put the specific audience or industry in the title

## Why NOT put the offer/CTA on informational pages
1. Intent mismatch: a "Get an Instant Quote" title on an answer-seeking query reads as an ad; users skip it for the result promising the answer. CTR drops.
2. Google rewrites boilerplate: Google rewrites well over half of title tags, and repetitive templated patterns are the top trigger. The same hook stamped on 1,700 pages gets flagged as boilerplate and swapped for scraped H1 text. Vary the hook by intent to stay under the rewrite radar.

## Examples
| Content type | Weak / competitor | BEG title |
|---|---|---|
| Payroll cost article (info) | Payroll Manager Salary Guide | What a Payroll Manager Really Costs in 2026 (Full Breakdown) \| BEG |
| Certified payroll guide (info) | Certified Payroll Guide | Certified Payroll, Explained: Rules, Forms, Penalties \| BEG |
| Legal hiring trend (info) | Attorney Hiring Trends | Why Attorney Searches Stall at 60 Days, and the Fix \| BEG |
| HCM / AI angle (info) | isolved AI Features | The First HR Platform That Does the Task, Not Just Answers \| BEG |
| Payroll hub (commercial) | Managed Payroll Services | Managed Payroll Services \| Instant Quote, $25-$45 PEPM \| BEG |
| Competitor page (commercial) | isolved vs Paycom | isolved vs Paycom \| Instant Price Quote, 330% ROI \| BEG |

## Execution + validation
- Commercial titles: shipped.
- Informational titles: rewrite blog/guide titles to the curiosity formula, rotating hook types. Commercial pages keep the offer hook. Do NOT apply the offer hook to informational pages.
- Once Google Search Console is connected, pull CTR-by-title and tune the hooks on real data, not theory. Kill hooks that Google is rewriting; double down on the ones lifting CTR.

## Hard don'ts
- No identical hook phrase across a large set of pages (rewrite risk).
- No CTA/offer language on informational titles.
- Never drop the ranking keyword to fit a hook.
