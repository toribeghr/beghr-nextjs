## Day 5

**Subject:** How to run a 23-35 day search, step by step

Hi [First Name],

Last one. Let us turn everything from this week into a playbook you can run on your next open role.

**1. Calibrate before you source (Days 1-2).** Get crisp on the must-haves versus the nice-to-haves, the comp range, and the one outcome this hire owns in year one. Most slow searches are slow because the target keeps moving.

**2. Go to passive candidates, do not wait for applicants (Days 2-10).** Source the people already doing the work somewhere else and reach out directly. Remember the window from Day 2. Lead with the role and the upside, not a generic pitch.

**3. Screen fast and keep the slate short (Days 8-15).** A focused slate of three to five genuinely qualified people beats a flood of fifty maybes. Speed here protects you from losing candidates to a faster competitor.

**4. Compress the interview loop (Days 14-25).** Decide your interview stages up front and book them tight. Days lost between rounds is where good candidates slip away. Treat every gap as risk.

**5. Move decisively to offer and close (Days 25-35).** When you find the right person, act before their window closes. A clean, prompt offer with clear next steps wins more candidates than the highest number that arrives a week late.

Run it this way and 23-35 days is realistic, not optimistic. The hard part is having the passive-candidate reach and the discipline to keep every stage moving, which is exactly what we do for clients every day.
